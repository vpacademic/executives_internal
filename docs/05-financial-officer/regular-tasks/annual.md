# Annual Tasks

Annual tasks span the full fiscal year (June 1–May 31). The single largest
annual task is the budget process, which runs February through May.

---

## Fiscal Year Calendar

| Month | Key task |
|-------|---------|
| June | New fiscal year begins; confirm budget is distributed |
| July | FO election at Council meeting |
| August | Outgoing FO transitional report due; incoming FO takes office (Aug 31) |
| September | First HFC meeting of new fiscal year |
| October–November | Monitor budget vs. actuals; trimester 1 ends |
| December | Capital asset audit (GM, first 2 weeks); HFC reviews audit report |
| January–February | Trimester 2 ends; begin budget review and timeline for next year |
| February | Send budget timeline + line template; all-candidate meeting |
| March | Meet elected executives re. preliminary goals |
| April | First and second departmental draft budgets to HFC |
| May | HFC approves final master budget; FO presents at May Council meeting |
| May 31 | Fiscal year ends; coordinate annual audit |

---

## Budget Process (February–May)

See [Budget Process](../governance/budget-process/) for the full breakdown.
The timeline below is from HF Policy Appendix II (Financial Budget Guideline).

### February
- The Accountant, HFC, GM, and FO review, discuss, and alter (if needed)
  the existing budget notes.
- The GM and FO send out a **new budget timeline** to all executives.
- The GM and FO meet outgoing executives for budget feedback and suggestions
  for the next year's budget.
- The GM and FO send out a **new budget line template**.
- The FO participates in the election's all-candidate meeting.

### March
- The FO and GM meet elected executives to ascertain their preliminary goals
  and how budgetary allocations would be affected.

### April
- Elected executives provide **first departmental draft budgets** to HFC
  (mid-April).
- HFC commences review of executive budgets.
- Elected executives provide **second departmental draft budgets** to HFC by
  end of April.

### May
- HFC reviews and discusses the second draft budgets.
- Executives make recommended changes.
- HFC approves the final master budget **at least one week before** the May
  Council meeting.
- **FO presents the final master budget** at the May Council meeting.
- Master budget is approved at the May Council meeting.

## Annual Audit (Post May 31)

**Your duty (Bylaw 8.5.6(c)):** Cause an audit of the financial records and
books of accounts to be performed at the end of each fiscal year.

In practice:
1. Coordinate with the GM and Accountant to ensure all year-end entries are
   complete.
2. Engage the external auditor (the GSS uses an external accountant for
   auditing; Bickert Management performs bookkeeping but not auditing).
3. Review the draft audit report with the Accountant.
4. Present audit results to Council.

---

## Capital Asset Audit (December)

The GM conducts a physical/remote audit of capital assets in the **first two
weeks of December**. The report is subsequently reviewed by the HFC.

Your role: ensure the audit takes place on schedule and review the report with
the GM before HFC presentation.

---

## Investment Account Review

The GSS holds investment accounts comprised of GICs and Mutual Funds — funds
not required in the short term.

Conduct an annual review of the investment portfolio in consultation with the
GM and the Society's investment manager.

{: .note }
As of the guide's last update, the GSS is in the process of transitioning its
investment manager to RBC Dominion Securities. Verify current status with the
GM before taking any action on investment accounts.

---

## AO Event Fund Administration

The GSS Event Fund (Policy 9) provides financial rebates to Affiliate
Organizations (AOs) for events or projects. The VP Students administers the
fund; the FO's role is to approve disbursements and ensure the fund operates
within its budget.

---

## Policy Manual Review

The HFC is responsible for an annual review of the House-Finance Manual to
ensure current practices align with policy (HF Policy 1.3). The FO, as the
responsible executive for HFC, facilitates this review.

The Executive Committee is also responsible for an annual review of the
Executive Policy (Executive Policy 1.3).
